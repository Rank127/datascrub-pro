## Post 4: Industry Insight

```
Unpopular opinion: Data brokers aren't evil.

They're just playing by rules that shouldn't exist.

Here's how the system works:

1. Public records are... public (by law)
2. Companies can aggregate public data (legal)
3. They can sell aggregated data (legal)
4. You have to opt-out site by site (by design)

The game is rigged against consumers.

CCPA and GDPR helped, but enforcement is weak.

Until the laws change, the best defense is opting out.

That's why I built GhostMyData - to automate what the law makes tedious.

Should data brokers be regulated more strictly?
```

## Tips for LinkedIn

1. **Post 2-3x per week** (not daily - quality over quantity)
2. **First line is everything** - make it hook
3. **Use line breaks** - walls of text don't work
4. **End with a question** - drives comments
5. **Reply to every comment** - within 1 hour if possible
6. **Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am or 5-6pm
7. **Add hashtags** at the end (3-5 max)
8. **Tag relevant people** sparingly (only if they'd genuinely care)
